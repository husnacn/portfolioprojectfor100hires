## Post 3
**Date:** April 12, 2026

**URL:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/retentionadam_i-have-24-stars-on-glassdoor-but-over-the-activity-7448790811904868352-OTbD?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAABvCC-8BaIYJGZPLSBP-LrMAx45vUNX38eE]

**Content**

I have 2.4 stars on Glassdoor. But over the last year, we've had near-zero voluntary employee attrition. Here's how I retain top talent:

1.  Focus on providing intellectual, interpersonal, and financial capital.

Forget the fancy office, the free lunches, and unlimited PTO. None of that matters. What matters is whether your people are doing the highest-level work possible, alongside the highest-level talent you can attract. And whether they're getting paid well enough to never entertain a recruiter's DM. Amazon can attract great talent without paying well. You can't.
 
2. Get an exec coach to teach you how to communicate clearly.

Two years ago I knew I was a horrible manager. I just didn't know the main skill I lacked was the ability to clearly define expectations and deliverables. Sounds simple. But if you spent 43 years not doing it properly, it's a hard habit to break. Jonathan Shapiro gets the credit for two years of hard work with our exec team, shaping us all into much better managers. Best money I've ever spent.
 
3.  Don’t squirrel.
 
Like any excitable founder, I used to get ideas and start working on them immediately. That works when you are 3 people, but not when you’re 30. Teams get frustrated when they're told to work on one thing Monday and something completely different by Thursday.

We rolled out EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System), and the single greatest benefit has been defining the work during quarterly planning sessions and refusing to deviate until the next quarter. 
 
4.  Give excessive recognition when good work is done.

Praising people for good work in a public setting is one of the most rewarding things you can do for your team members. Any time a shout-out is given in a smaller setting, make sure the exec team hears it, and shout out as many people as you can during all hands meetings. 

5. Every once in a while, do something fun.

I haven't done this, but if you asked each of our 35 remote team members what their favorite work moment of the last 12 months was, my guess is over half would say it was either our Cancun or New York offsites. Our global team gets together in person twice per year, stays in nice places, does fun stuff, and eats great food.  Yes, we're bootstrapped. Yes, it's expensive. But I know this small thing is a big reason people stick around.

TAKEAWAY

Finding and keeping a talented employees is like finding and keeping a spouse.

If you want to find a good spouse, you have to first live a life that is worthy of a good spouse.

If you want to find and keep good talent, your company has to be worthy of good talent.

What did I miss?

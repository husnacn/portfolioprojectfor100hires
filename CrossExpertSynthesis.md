## Ideas that repeat across experts

### 1) Clarity > activity

- **Adam**: define expectations, avoid “squirreling,” stick to quarterly plans
- **Ashley**: clear roles, clear plans, avoid last-minute chaos
- **Chris**: execution without clarity = wasted effort

**Shared idea**: Most problems aren’t effort problems—they’re clarity problems.

### 2) Systems thinking over hero effort

- **Adam**: retention comes from systems (planning, recognition, comp), not perks
- **Ashley**: “hero work” = bad planning
- **Chris**: overworking reduces performance and is outdated

**Shared idea**: If people need to “try harder,” the system is broken.

### 3) Skepticism toward traditional “best practices”

- **Adam**: challenges standard GTM (e.g., no long qualification, instant demos)
- **Ashley**: rejects rigid funnels, pushes flexible journeys
- **Chris**: explicitly says best practices are often obsolete

**Shared idea**: Default playbooks are often outdated—context matters more.

### 4) AI is powerful—but not for everything

- **Adam**: AI for execution, not strategy
- **Ashley**: AI must be integrated carefully (data, workflows, experimentation)
- **Chris**: AI replaces knowledge work; human edge shifts elsewhere

**Shared idea**: AI = leverage tool, not a thinking replacement.

### 5) Human factors matter more than tools

- **Adam**: communication, recognition, team quality
- **Ashley**: communication rituals, trust, team health
- **Chris**: identity, subconscious, emotional regulation

**Shared idea**: Performance is driven more by humans (mindset, communication, identity) than tools or tactics.

### 6) Speed + focus as competitive advantage

- **Adam**: speed in GTM, fast access, no friction
- **Ashley**: stick to plan, reduce chaos, align teams
- **Chris**: clarity → faster, better decisions

**Shared idea**: The faster and more focused you are, the better you perform.

## Where they disagree

These aren’t contradictions—they’re different philosophies.

### 1) Role of AI in thinking

- **Adam Robinson**
  - AI is risky for thinking (bias, overconfidence)
  - prefers human strategic thinking
- **Chris Walker**
  - problem isn’t AI, it’s your subconscious
  - thinking quality depends on internal state, not tools
- **Ashley Faus**
  - practical middle: integrate AI into workflows carefully

**Tension**
- **Adam**: limit AI in thinking
- **Chris**: fix the thinker, not the tool
- **Ashley**: operationalize AI safely

### 2) Source of performance improvement

- **Adam**: better systems (planning, hiring, GTM)
- **Ashley**: better execution + collaboration + planning
- **Chris**: subconscious identity is the root of everything

**Tension**
- **Adam/Ashley**: external systems
- **Chris**: internal wiring

**Philosophical split**: “Fix the system” vs “Fix the person”.

### 3) View on discipline & effort

- **Adam**: doesn’t emphasize discipline; focuses on structure
- **Ashley**: effort is fine, but overwork = bad management
- **Chris**: discipline is fundamentally flawed

**Tension**
- **Chris**: anti-discipline
- **Others**: anti-bad-systems causing overwork

### 4) Strategy vs experimentation

- **Adam**: validate ideas fast, build in public, move quickly
- **Ashley**: plan carefully, stick to plan
- **Chris**: ignore noise, follow vision

**Tension**
- **Adam**: rapid experimentation
- **Ashley**: structured execution
- **Chris**: vision-driven conviction
